Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about USSD integration in Liberia

What is USSD?

+

USSD (Unstructured Supplementary Service Data) is a protocol used by GSM cellular telephones to communicate with service provider computers. It's the technology behind codes like *123# that allow users to access services without internet connectivity.

Do I need to contact every telco individually?

+

No! That's exactly what Access Link solves. Instead of managing relationships with Orange, MTN, and Lonestar separately, you integrate once with our platform and reach all networks automatically.

Can I get my own shortcode?

+

Yes! We offer both shared shortcodes for quick deployment and dedicated shortcodes for brand recognition. Dedicated codes require LTA approval, which we help facilitate.

How long does integration take?

+

With shared shortcodes, you can be live within 24-48 hours. Dedicated shortcodes typically take 2-4 weeks depending on LTA approval timelines. Our team handles all regulatory requirements.

Is the platform compliant with LTA regulations?

+

Absolutely. Access Link is fully compliant with all Liberia Telecommunications Authority regulations. We maintain all required licenses and handle regulatory reporting on your behalf.
